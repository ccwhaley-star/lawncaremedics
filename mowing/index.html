Professional Lawn Mowing in Gretna & Omaha

Clean cuts, sharp edges, reliable weekly service

A mowed lawn isn't just about how it looks — it's about how healthy the grass stays. Cut too short, too often, or with dull blades and you stress the lawn instead of maintaining it. Our mowing service is built around what actually keeps Nebraska lawns thriving: proper cut height, clean edges, and consistent weekly service from April through October.

What's Included in Every Visit

  • Mowing at optimal height for Nebraska cool-season grasses (3–3.5")
  • Trimming around fences, house, trees, and landscaping
  • Edging where grass meets driveways and sidewalks
  • Blowing all clippings off driveways, sidewalks, and patios
  • Sharp blades, every time — dull blades tear grass, sharp blades cut it cleanly

How Often We Mow

Weekly service is standard during peak growing season (April through October). Biweekly is available for specific situations or off-season trim-ups. We put you on an automatic schedule so you never have to call and ask when we're coming.

Rain delays are handled automatically. If we can't mow safely on your scheduled day, we'll move you to the next dry day — usually within 24–48 hours.

Pricing

Standard residential mowing starts at $40 per visit, with the final rate based on your lot size, gate access, and any add-on services. Every quote is free — no contracts, no hidden fees.

Why Cut Height Matters in Nebraska

Scalping a lawn (cutting too short) is the single most common cause of thin, weedy turf in the Omaha metro. At 3" or taller:

  • Grass shades out crabgrass and broadleaf weeds naturally
  • Deeper roots develop, making the lawn more drought-tolerant
  • Soil stays cooler and retains moisture longer through summer

We follow the "one-third rule" — never removing more than ⅓ of the grass blade in a single cut — which is the standard from UNL Extension and every other turf authority.
