Why Lawn Care Is Different in Gretna

Two things define Gretna lawns:

  • Clay-heavy soil with shallow topsoil. Most new construction in Gretna strips topsoil during grading, leaving lawns to start on compacted clay subsoil. That is why so many yards look thin by their second summer.
  • Cool-season grass (mostly Kentucky bluegrass). These grasses have peak growth phases in spring and fall, go semi-dormant in August heat, and need very specific timing on fertilizer and aeration to thrive.

Generic national lawn care programs do not account for this. Our 6-step fertilization program and fall aeration/over-seeding schedule are timed to Gretna actual soil temperatures, not a national calendar.
